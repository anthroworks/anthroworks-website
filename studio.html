AnthroWorks Studio

Nine apps.
One thesis.

AnthroWorks Studio builds consumer AI apps rooted in cultural knowledge, indigenous naming systems, and anthropological thinking. Each app follows a 90-day build cadence. Design-led. Corpus-backed. Built to compound.

The Thesis

AI products built on cultural depth create moats that pure technology cannot.

Every Studio app is built on a curated corpus of indigenous knowledge, cultural context, and anthropological research. This isn’t a feature — it’s the foundation. While competitors race to ship generic AI wrappers, we invest in the data layer that makes our products irreplaceable.

The result: apps that don’t just work, but resonate. Products that honour the knowledge systems they draw from. Technology rooted in something deeper than a trend cycle.

Flagship — App 01

IZWI

Nature identification. Cultural knowledge.

Point your camera at any plant, bird, insect, or animal — IZWI identifies it and returns its indigenous names, cultural significance, traditional uses, and ecological context alongside the scientific data. Powered by Claude Haiku and a curated cultural corpus that no competitor can replicate.

IZWI means “voice” in isiZulu. It gives voice to the knowledge systems that Western field guides have always left out.
